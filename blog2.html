Margins, Borders and Padding

All elements on an HTML page is essentially contained inside a rectangle or box. These boxes/rectangles have the 3 properties: Margin, Border and Padding. These 3 properties change all the visuals aspects of such element, insdie the box/rectangle.

Margin

With the box model there are 3 layers to every box. The Outer layer is the Margin, like the wrapping surrounding a paper. The Margin allows you to add spacing between the outside of the border and any of the other element with boxes/borders. The Margin can be changed on any side of the boxing individually, which gives it great potential to move all the elements around page.

Border

The border is the box of the element. It is like the physical box containing the element, you can change its thickness, positioning and even color. It is incredibly useful and can be changed in many different ways to add great variety to your work.

Padding

An element's padding area is the space between its content and its border. The padding can be controlled on any side such as the border. Its like filling the inside between a product and its boxing, such as bublerap.
